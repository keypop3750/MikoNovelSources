Time goes back to before the battle in the labyrinth began. 
 
 
As soon as the leaders of the [Demon Country], Tempest, gathered at the meeting, decided on a course of action, Testarossa made her move quickly. 
 
As soon as I leave the control room, I send Cien, who I left behind in the Kingdom of Inglacia, a Thought Communication. After briefly relaying the contents of the meeting, I order her to discuss the rest with Hinata and decide. 
 
Next, just as I was about to contact Moss, I was greeted by Soka with a stern expression. 
 
「Oh?」 
 
「I'll show you the way.」 
 
「Do you not need to help Mr. Soei?」 
 
「Yes. My subordinates have been sent to various places.」 
 
Soka said, and nodded lightly. 
 
That was the truth. 
 
In the Holy Cloistered Kingdom of Rubelios, Hokuso. 
 
In the Holy Void, in Damargania, Sayaka. 
 
Sarion, the Sorcerer's Dynastic Dynasty, has Nanso.

And where we were headed now, Touka was on a mission, but we had lost contact with her. 
 
Judging by the situation, they’d probably been shut off from the world of ice Velzard had created 
 
If his subordinate, Touka, had turned into an ice sculpture, then it was only natural that 
Soka would participate in this rescue operation

Even if he hadn’t explained everything, Testarossa could guess what was going on. 
 
Following Soka’s lead, the two of them headed for the teleportation-exclusive isolation room. 
 
It was forbidden to “transfer” directly from within the labyrinth to the outside in order to prevent the enemy from infiltrating. It would be a pain for those who could “spatial transfer” freely, but everyone was following this rule to follow the safety-first policy.

Besides, she could understand Soka’s desire to help her subordinates who were caught up in it.

On the other hand, it's because he became an ice sculpture that there's a possibility he's still alive.

It would be difficult for them to even control their primogenitor, but it was impossible for Testarossa to control the “dragon species” that reigned at the top of the mental lifeforms in perfect condition. 
 
There was an example, so the possibility wasn’t zero, but it made more sense to judge that it was all Velzard’s will

But Testarossa was glaring at him, thinking that wasn’t all.

If she had an answer, there was a reason to worry, but if not, it would be a waste of time to think about it. 
 
That would be the best way to decipher Velzard’s intentions, but even Testarossa couldn’t perfectly see the mind of a “dragon species.” 
 
Either way, there was no doubt that it was best not to cause unnecessary casualties. In that case, I should have used as many safety measures as I could to tackle the mission. 
 
“Moss, come here.” 
 
“Understood.”

「You're going with me, aren't you?」 
 
「Yes. My mission is to investigate the local situation. If possible, I'd like to rescue the rescuers.」

「Huh?」

「--?! Then it's too late?!」 
 
「No, that's not it.」 
 
“Calm down,” Testarossa said, restraining Soka. 
 
Then, in a stuttering voice, she explained the current situation so that even Soka could understand. 
 
「It's only stopped, so we can resume.」 
 
「Huh?」 
 
「In other words, it wasn't an attack. In fact...」 
 
That's right. If anything, that ice and snow is like a protective curtain protecting that land. 
 
In ancient times, Testarossa had witnessed Velzard fighting Gii. 
I’d been spying on the scenery on the ground from the demon realms, but the battle had been an overwhelming one 
 
Velzard's Air Prison Wall, which congealed the atmosphere, easily blocked Gii's attack. But the next moment, it was shattered by a barrage of magic. 
 
The scene repeats itself. 
 
The exchange of transcendent techniques told me that both sides were on another level. 
 
At the time, she hadn't even been able to probe the weaknesses of both of them... However, the current Testarossa could at least understand the nature of those techniques. 
 
Velzard’s ice and snow had put all things still. 
 
Most likely, they’re using the same principle as the Air Prison Wall, which was a defensive technique, to extend the effect time by freezing it. 
 
Even if I used [Air Prison Wall] to capture my opponent normally, it would be dispelled in a short time, so it wouldn't be suited for sealing and the like. However, in this state combined with the ice, I should be able to restrict its movement semipermanently. 
 
It was like a prison of ice where even consciousness had been stolen, and even considering that not even Carrera could escape, its strength and precision had reached a frightening level. 
 
Moreover, it was originally a defensive technique~ 
 
「----I think they're protecting you so that it's safe to get involved in their own battles.」 
 
「Umm, eh?」 
 
「There's no doubt that ice statues are difficult to destroy.」 
 
That was what Testarossa declared. 
 
Belatedly, a look of understanding finally appeared on Soka's face. 
 
「Does that mean Master Velzard has no hostile intent?」 
 
That was a difficult question. 
 
Testarossa agonized over it for a moment before answering. 
 
「Rather than an enemy, Lord Velzard must have some kind of plan. He must have taken such measures because he doesn't want to be disturbed.」 
 
It wasn’t that he was being controlled by Feldway, it was just that their interests aligned. And Velzard had probably created a situation that was convenient for him in order to achieve that goal. 
 
Testarossa recounted those inferences. 
 
「Does that mean the rescue operation itself is unnecessary?」 
 
「Yeah, you're right. Actually, you probably can't move it.」 
 
「--?」 
 

Seeing that Soka was beyond comprehension, Testarossa supplemented her explanation. 
 
「It wasn't just the life-forms that were frozen by that ice and snow. The surface of the earth itself, part of the surface of this planet, is protected so that it won't break under the attack of Lord Velzard and the others.」 
 
「I see...」 
 
Soka finally understood. 
 
-No, that's not it. 
 
I understood, but the scale was so different that it didn't feel real at all. 
 
Still, he had a grasp of the situation, so he understood what Testarossa was trying to say 
 
(It's hard to believe that everyone was turned into ice statues in order to protect the planet, but... If that's the truth, then we don't need to carry the ice statue to a safe place.) 
 
“Ah, so that’s why~” Soka understood. 
 
It's not that I don't need to. I can't move it. 
If everything had been fixed in place, the ice statues would have become one, and they wouldn’t be able to move from that place. That was what Testarossa’s words meant.

If rescue operations were meaningless, then the only thing Soka could do would be reconnaissance missions. And if she had to jump into a hellish place where the White Ice Dragon Velzard and the Demon Lord Gii were fighting at full strength, it was inevitable that she wouldn’t even be able to guarantee Soka’s own safety. 
 
Is it really worth the risk? 
 
Soka nods. 
 
「If you will allow it, I will go as well.」 
 
Soka is also stealthy. 
 
As Soei’s confidant, he had a duty to tell the central government what was happening there 
 
There's no need for others to realize that, but Soei is pushing himself. It's precisely because Soka has been chasing Soei's back for so long that he realizes that fact. 
 
「You might die, you know?」 
 
「I'm prepared!」 
 
Soka answered Testarossa’s question without hesitation. 
 
Her resolve is beautiful. 
 
Testarossa smiled in satisfaction. 
 
「Honestly, there isn't much you can do on-site. Even we can do it if we just report the situation to the control room.」 
 
「......」 
 
「But there's a role only you can play.」 
 
「What in the world...?」 
 
「It's a marker.」 
 
「A landmark?」 
 
「Yes, that’s right. In the battle between Sir Velzard and Gii, that area has been transformed into an unobservable danger zone. It’s impossible to ‘transfer’ directly to a place like that, and naturally, we’ll have to jump a little ways away from it that isn’t affected.」 
 
Soka nods in agreement. 
 
However, after hearing Testarossa’s next words, he came to understand her intentions. 
 
「But you know, if someone I knew had been there, I would have been able to jump towards that person.」 
 
That was what Testarossa had said. 
 
Gii, Velzard, and Testarossa were well aware of both. It was possible to use those two as a guide to perform a “spatial transfer,” but in that case, if they were unlucky, there was a chance they would be caught up in a transcendental attack. 
 
Since we don't know what's going on in the area, the situation right after the transfer is left up to luck. As expected, that's nothing short of suicide from Testarossa's point of view. In other words, it's a dangerous act for everyone. 
 
In that regard, if there was someone observing the situation on-site... 
 
「I see. So just the fact that I'm alive guarantees that the place is a safe zone...」 
 
「Exactly.」 
 
Testarossa smiled in satisfaction at Soka’s perceptiveness. 
 
It was a gentle smile, but very cold and ruthless. 
After all, those words were as good as telling Soka to become a canary in the coal mine. 
 
What’s more, it was a job that even Soka could do. 
 
As long as you live on the spot, anyone is fine. 
 
Besides, the only time they’d be able to take that much risk would be the small amount of time they could buy when reinforcements arrived... 
 
Under normal circumstances, there was no need to take such a risk. 
 
But Soka had already made up her mind. 
 
「There is no problem. If I can be of any use to you, I will simply carry out that mission.」 
 
With a single unclouded look in her eyes, Soka declared that. 
 
(I mustn't be Master Soei's confidant if he thinks I'm useless. I'll deal with him, including the situation report!) 
 
That resolve was more than enough to move Testarossa’s heart 
 
「It's fine. It's wonderful, Soka-san.」 
 
Testarossa had reevaluated Soka: Rimuru’s subordinates all had strong wills, but Soka was just as wonderful as that example. 
 
Testarossa’s smile deepened. 
 
Then, she directed her order at the empty space. 
 
「Moss, be sure to protect Soka-san.」 
 
Soka looked over to see Moss kneeling there, having come at the behest of her. 
 
She bowed respectfully in response to his request. 
 
「Umm...?」 
 
Soka panicked, thinking that if Moss was here, she wouldn't need her role anymore. 
 
However, neither Testarossa nor Moss were fazed. 
 
「It's only natural for me, but even Moss can erase his presence. It'll be difficult for even the top brass to find out where he is.」 
 
Diablo could probably do it. However, Testarossa assured him that it would be difficult for anyone else. 
 
Soka thought she should be able to detect Moss's presence, but when she thought about it carefully, she realized that there weren't many high-ranking members who were close to him. 
 

If it's Soei, he's using Moss as well, but that's just a one-way trip from Mos. The same goes for Benimaru. He only receives information from Moth. 
 
In the first place, for a Demon, a spiritual life-form who excels at covert operations, using their own locational information to trickle out is foolishness itself. Trying to convince them to stay here is like unbearable torture. 
 
He’d have to do it if it was necessary, but he really didn’t want to do anything conspicuous in front of Velzard. 
 
「If I unnaturally show off my presence, Velzard-sama might think I'm annoying and target me first...」 
 
And Moss whispered in a low voice loud enough for only Soka to hear. 
 
“I see.” Soka agreed, and decided to accept Moss’s escort. 
 
And so, the party made their way to the frigid hell where Velzard’s ice and snow raged.

Don't die. It was an impossible task in this situation.

Both Gii and Velzard were more powerful than Testarossa. If they got caught up in a fight like that, there was a good chance they would die without knowing anything.

Velzard’s ice and snow had swallowed everything up and painted it all in a single color.

After all, she is the Primogenitor's White Bran, the ruthless, cold, and beautiful White Queen to all her enemies.

We're almost at the heart of the matter, but I stop thinking there. 
 
Unlike normal people, they don't act without conviction. That's what Testarossa is.

Despite its unparalleled power, it had little use for it. Even Testarossa, who excelled at intellect, hadn’t been able to use this power.

This synergistic effect was beyond imagination, giving Testarossa an absolute advantage that made anyone who opposed her feel pitiful. 
 
Moss, who was the White Faction’s viceroy, was the only one who was well aware of the horrors of his power. 
Life can't be lived in hell. It's a true emptiness that can destroy even souls and turn them into energy. 
 
Only high-ranking spiritual life-forms can withstand this hell... 
 
And the abyss of hell was a realm where even those higher beings couldn’t survive. 
 
Testarossa's Pure White World, the Nihilistic World, can summon that abyss into the real world. 
 
Incidentally, Ultima’s specialty Dark Magic: Void Vanishment Prison, or Nihilistic Vanish, was a magic mimicking the workings of this hellish abyss. Since this was unfolding to cover not only a wide area, but an entire region, it made sense how dangerous this was. 
 
Even if Testarossa failed to control it, the “emptiness” would overflow indefinitely, and the world would be swallowed up by the ever-expanding abyss and collapse 
 
Moss knew that. 
 
With only Testarossa’s will, death would come that would not allow her to resist. The speed at which the white space unfolded surpassed even the speed of light. 
 
I have to run away right now. 
 
Before Testarossa could unleash her power... 
 
If they got caught up in it, Moss would die instantly, too. 
 
It's not about protecting Soka, it's about letting go of your own life. 
 
Moreover, it was thought that even resurrection might be impossible. 
 
I've never tried it before, and I don't want to try it, so the truth is still unknown, but... As far as Moss is concerned, it doesn't matter either way. I don’t care if I remain ignorant until the end of my eternal life. 
 

With that thought in mind, I tried to act quickly, but that was when I suddenly reconsidered that maybe Moss wasn’t. 
 
Testarossa’s Pure White World was terrifying, but it wasn’t invincible. Not only was it difficult to set the conditions, but even if she activated it, she wouldn’t be able to defeat an overwhelmingly superior level. 
 
If they were on the same level as Testarossa, like Carrera and Ultima, victory and defeat would be decided without issue. However, when the opponent became a “dragon species,” it would be difficult to make that vast amount of energy disappear. 
 
Not to mention, there are gii in this land. 
 
Gii, the strongest of the seven primogenitors, was definitely stronger than Testarossa. 
 
(If it was just Lord Gii, I might be able to defeat him, but...) 
With Gii and Velzard around, there was no telling how far the effects of the Pure White 
World and the Nihilistic World would extend 
 
Testarossa wasn't the type to take bets. She was intelligent and cautious, like she was assessing the tide of battle and seeking the optimal solution appropriately. 
 
The reason Testarossa unleashed her power here and now was... 
 
(Gii-sama and Lord Velzard are the only ones in this land, right? No, is that really true...?) 
 
At this point, Moss finally realized that the premise might have been wrong. 
 
Like Testarossa, Moss is gathering information. 
 
I cast off my own “bundling” to scrutinize the area. I shared that information with Testarossa, too, so I didn’t see anything out of the ordinary. 
 
That should have been the case, but there was a possibility I had overlooked it.

A stinging time passed, and Gii escaped Velzard’s “absolute stop.” 
 
If he hadn't understood that power, his defeat would have been decided the moment he stopped moving. 
 
It was a power that was too reckless to rely on brute force to stop its kinetic energy by exerting its influence on all possible substances. 
 
It was because it was reckless that I was able to repel it with the force of my will. It was a feat unique to Gii, but it wasn't that exhausting, so there was no problem. 
 
Besides, that time was precious. 
 
By observing Velzard the whole time, Gii had gained a certain conviction. 
 
There was no doubt that Velzard was acting from the heart, but there was a good chance he’d been tempted by someone. 
 
Not to mention the Twilight that dwells in Velzard. 
 
Velzard himself seemed to think he was acting of his own free will, but he thought it was the result of cleverly guiding him. 
 
(That bastard probably wants to watch me and Velzard fight a real battle and use it for his own theory.) 
 
Velzard was no fool, but he had a single-minded side to him. To clever Twilight, deceiving him must have been as simple as twisting a baby’s hand. 
 
The trouble is that this has nothing to do with the influence of power and so on. 
 
If he didn’t move Velzard’s heart, Gii’s words wouldn’t reach him 
 
That would be very troublesome, and from Gii’s perspective, it would be a gamble with little chance of victory. 
 
That's why I couldn't get over it, so I kept watching her... 
 
I had to make a decision soon. 
 
He couldn’t leave Velzard alone any longer. 
 
And more than anything, I have a bad feeling about this. 
 
Twilight is the worst crime of all time. He's a villain who has come up with every possible plan, and whether it succeeds or fails, he's the one who spreads malice throughout the world. 
 
In the past, there was a side of me that I couldn't hate more than I thought... but one day, I started experimenting inhumanely, as if someone had changed. 
 
(Dagrull was lamenting too, wasn't he? In the end, he didn't tell me what happened. Well, it's got nothing to do with me.) 
 
Even if something had happened to an old acquaintance, as long as he laid his hands on something precious to him, he was still an enemy to Gii. 
 
Gii quietly grew more and more angry. 
 
It served as fuel, burning Gii's core. 
 
(Nonsense. Indestructible? A power that can't be realized without relying on others is unworthy.) 
 
It was up to Twilight to fulfill his own desires, so he was free to do as he wished. However, it was extremely unpleasant to hear that he had been used by his kin―his partner. 
 
Whether Velzard was deceived or not was up to Gii's discretion. However, Gii was more angry than ever before, so it didn't matter what the truth was. 
 
Gii unleashed his Elemental Magic: [Heat Dragon Flame Conqueror], Napalm Burst. The scorching dragon wriggled its long torso and danced wildly around Velzard. 
 
It was a spell that burned through the target just by touching it, but he had already assumed it wouldn't work on Velzard. Gii had used it for restraint from the start. 
 
However, this was also pointless. 
 
It had been scattered by the [Beautiful Thin Ice] [Diamond Dust] created as soon as 
Velzard breathed in, and it had disappeared with a large ring of fire blooming in the air. 
 
[Ice Breath] [Freezing Breath]. It's an absolute freezing wave emitted in dragon form, but Velzard can be used even in human form. 
 
Gii clicked his tongue inwardly. 
 
At the same time, I was impressed. 
 
(She really is strong!) 
 
Gii smiled happily. 
 
Velzard was unbelievably strong, as Gii had acknowledged. His power hadn't waned since they first met. On the contrary, he was no less powerful than Gii, who had continued to grow in strength, and they were competing to the same level as him. 
 
Gii was greatly satisfied that he was worthy of being his partner. He was worried about having to do something about such an opponent, but that only made him more motivated. 
 
It's standard practice to say that those without emotions or wills are weak. 
The reason Twilight had lost to Testarossa was probably because she’d abandoned that emotion 
 
If I kill my emotions, I'll be left behind from the change. 
 
That’s why those without wills don’t grow. 
 
In that regard, Velzard was different. 
 
If Velzard was just running amok under someone else’s control, there was no way he could reach this level of strength. It was precisely because he was steeped in a one-pointspecific form of “jealousy” that he truly had become supremely strong. 
 
(Is Twilight using him as a stepping stone to feed himself? Well, either way, we have to get rid of that Twilit bastard.) 
 
Gii intends to deal with Twilight regardless of whether Velzard returns to his senses or not. 
 
(Good grief. Looks like I was overthinking things. If I think about things simply, I can see the answer!) 
 
Gii cast aside his idle thoughts and focused solely on the problem at hand. 
 
If Velzard is being selfish, then Gii is the embodiment of arrogance. Everyone acts as they please, with the spirit of crushing others because they don't like them. 
 
Once she remembered that it was her, the rest was simple. All she had to do was explode her pent-up anger and go wild as her heart desired. 
 
At this point, Gii finally got serious. 
 
He stopped leaving his remaining strength behind in preparation for the Ivarage and faced Velzard with all his might. 
 
 
＊ 
 
“Ah, so you’ve finally gotten serious.” Velzard rejoiced. 
 
With this, he could finally enjoy fighting in a world where he was alone with Gii. Just thinking about it made Velzard smile naturally. 
 
(Still, Gii really is strong.) 
 
The battle had begun, and the date had already changed. Neither of them had been at full strength, but the battle had taken place in a realm where no ordinary demon lord could enter. 
 
Gii didn't show any openings. 
 
She was acting as an example, as if she could see through our plan. 
 
Besides, Gii had gathered magicules from the atmosphere and used magic just like humans did. In other words, he was fighting using Velzard’s [Magic] [Energy], so he hadn’t worn himself down at all. 
 
Normally, this would be an inefficient method of casting magic, but things would be different if Gii did it. He could instantly convert magicules into magic without even creating a slight time lag. 
 
Gii is strong. 
 
He was the strongest of the Primogenitor, and even if Velzard were to go all out to defeat him, he wouldn’t know if he could win. 
 
Velzard, who had been by Gii’s side all this time, knew why Gii was the strongest. 
 
Gii's genius observation allowed him to understand the true nature of the skill just by looking at a glimpse of it. 
 
As such, all of Velzard’s tricks were as good as exposed. If all he had were Ultimate Abilities, Ultimate Skills, and Gabriel, the King of Patience, his chances of victory would have been close to zero. 
 
That was why Velzard had warmed his inner hand for this day. 
 
The hand called Twilight had been sealed by Testarossa’s intervention. 
 
I only left my trump card, Leviathan, behind, but it looks like Gii found out about this too. 
“I really can’t let my guard down, can I?” Velzard grew irritated, and was just as happy. 
 
That's why I'm so proud of being the only one who acknowledged it. 
 
But I can't just be happy. 
 
In terms of [Magic] [Energy], Velzard is twice as strong as Gii, but there's only one trick left. It's because we only have one chance to win. 
 
Right now, I’m using [Frozen World] to circulate magicules, so it’s not wearing me down at all. But now that Gii’s serious, I should’ve assumed he’d lost that advantage. 
 
But I couldn't let my impatience get the better of me. If I let my skills show, my chances of victory would be squashed. 
 
Just like before, I had to drive Gii into a corner with “The King of Patience” and then use “Leviathan” for my special attack. 
 
(This is why I should have prepared more trump cards before I had to deal with Gii.) 
 
Twilight's pseudo-dragon form didn't even serve as a diversion. Luck doesn't seem to be on Velzard's side, but that's just fate. 
 
Gii had gotten serious, so his goal was as good as accomplished. 
 
Velzard flew toward Gii with all his might. 
And so, Gii and Velzard--the ones at the top of the world--finally got serious.
